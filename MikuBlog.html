About this Page

Welcome to the blog dedicated to vocaloid and its most famous character, Hatsune Miku. In this space you'll dive into her history, her global influence, and from her origins as a vocal synthesizer to a global phenomenom that has captivated hearts of millions since 2007.

You'll also discover the best games featuring her and understand how Vocaloid has revolutionized music creation. We'll also explore other Vocaloids that have contributed to expanding this phenomenom. Whether you're a longtime fan or just discovering her music and influence, we invite you to explore, learn, and celebrate the creativity that Miku has brought to millions across the world.

Vocaloid

Vocaloid is a voice synthesis software that enables musicians and producers to create songs using virtual voices instead of relying on human singers. The process involves recording a variety of vocal samples from real singers, which are then processed into a digital format that allows users to control every aspect of the voice. These virtual singers can be programmed to sing specific lyrics, melodies, and harmonies, with users controlling pitch, tone, tempo, and even emotional expression.

Each voice in Vocaloid is personified by a character, like Hatsune Miku, who has become a virtual idol with her own fanbase. These characters aren’t just tools for creating music, but they also function as performers in their own right, often featured in concerts as holographic projections and in collaborations with real-world artists.

What makes Vocaloid particularly appealing is its accessibility for independent artists. Without the need to hire a singer, musicians can produce and release fully vocalized tracks, even if they don’t have the budget or resources for professional recording sessions. This has democratized music production and opened up new opportunities for creativity.

The Vocaloid community is another important factor. Musicians from all over the world share their creations, remix each other’s work, and explore new genres. This collaborative environment has led to the emergence of entirely new musical styles and helped virtual singers gain a cultural following.

Ultimately, Vocaloid offers both technical and creative freedom, allowing artists to produce professional-quality vocal performances without the limitations that come with live singers. It’s a tool that blends technology, music, and virtual performance, making it a groundbreaking platform in modern music production.

Vocaloid offers a level of precision and flexibility that human voices can’t always provide. Producers can fine-tune every note and syllable to fit their artistic vision.

About Miku

Hatsune Miku was one of the first character personas developed by Crypton Future Media for their Vocaloid software.

She debuted in 2007. Her name is from the Japanese characters for first, sound, and future, so her name means “first sound from the future.” According to her backstory, Hatsune Miku comes from a not-so-distant future where music was lost. She emerged as a 16-year-old singing android who reintroduced the skills to the world.

Part of the reason that Hatsune Miku is so popular is that she was created to be collaborative. The original illustrations of her are licensed under Creative Commons, and there’s a large community dedicated to creating and sharing songs for her. So far, Crypton Future Media boasts that over 100,000 songs have been produced using her vocal software.

There is no "official" artist, label, band, or anything for Miku, instead, songs became so viral that they established themselves as classics, but every day people from around the globe makes and releases songs with Miku (even I fiddled with her voice some years ago). The thing is that the voice is so prominent on those songs, that the community treats it like if she was the artist, as we often hear the song for the use of the voicebank rather than the artist behind. That being said, there are some established Vocaloid producers, each one with it's own style, but all using Miku and/or other vocaloid voicebanks as their main "instrument.

Other Vocaloid

Gumi Vocaloid is a well-known virtual singer and one of the most popular characters in the Vocaloid software series. With her unique voice and captivating performances, Gumi has captured the hearts of both casual listeners and hardcore fans alike.

She is one of the most popular Vocaloid after Miku, she holds the record of the most viewed Vocaloid song on Youtube. The song "Echo" by CircusP actually has 109 Million views on Youtube.

Kasane Teto is NOT a vocaloid like the other ones in this list. She is an Utauloid, a free version of the Vocaloid software. Utau is a website where everyone can make their own voicebank. Teto is the most popular Utau nowadays.

She started as an April Fools Joke as a parody of Hatsune Miku until she got so popular that got an official SynthV version.

Kaai Yuki was worked under the concept to faithfuly copy the voice of little girls. Her voicebank was provided by a elementary school girl and for legal reasons her name has never been revealed.

She is the only known vocaloid that her voice is entirely by a little girl.

Megurine Luka is the product and character name of the speech synthesis and desktop music (DTM) software that was released by Crypton Future Media on January 30.

Luka was the first female bilingual Vocaloid, the first to have an English voicebank provided by a Japanese company, and the first Vocaloid2 by Crypton Future Media to be upgraded to the Vocaloid4 engine.

Games

The Hatsune Miku: Project DIVA series is a rhythm game franchise developed by SEGA featuring the popular virtual idol Hatsune Miku, powered by the Vocaloid software. Launched in 2009, the games allow players to perform interactive music videos with button prompts timed to songs performed by Miku and other Vocaloid characters like Kagamine Rin and Len, Megurine Luka, and Kaito. Known for its vibrant visuals, customization options, and wide song selection, *Project DIVA* has become a major cultural icon in both Japan and globally, celebrated for showcasing Vocaloid music and fan-made content.

I feel like this series and its Music Videos are the responsible of the viralization on Miku here in this part of the globe.

The Project Sekai: Colorful Stage! feat. Hatsune Miku, is a mobile rhythm game collaboration between SEGA, Craft Egg, and Crypton Future Media. Released in 2020, it brings together Vocaloid characters like Hatsune Miku and original, fully voiced characters in a story-driven experience. Players can perform rhythm games with button prompts to songs across various genres, exploring the stories and challenges of different music-themed units. With frequent updates, events, and customizable performances, *Project Sekai* has become popular for blending Vocaloid music with original content and connecting fans globally.

This mobile game has led to the viralization of various songs on Tiktok and has become an easy accesible platform to know vocaloid songs.
